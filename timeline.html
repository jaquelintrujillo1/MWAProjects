The U.S. Department of Defense announced that 3,700 members of the military would be sent to the southern border. They were sent there to assist Customs and Border Protection. Before the deployment, there were nearly 650 troops at the border.President Trump innitially wanted to send troops to the board on April 4, 2018 Trump says that they were deployed to stop "a drastic surge of illegal activity on the southern border."

January 19, 2019

Trump releases plan to secure border and end partial shutdown

President Trump created a plan to secure the southern boarder and end the government's partial shutdown

Trump's plain included:

  • $5.7 billion to fund a steel barrier system
  • $805 million for technology, canines, and personnel to prevent drugs and weapons from being brought into the country
  • $800 million dollars in humanitarian assistance, medical support, and new temporary housing
  • $782 million to hire an additional 2,750 border agents, law enforcement officers, and staff
  • $563 million for the immigration court system, including hiring 75 new immigration judge teams
  • Three years of provisional status for DACA and certain Temporary Protected Status (TPS) recipients, which would protect them from deportation.

June 11, 2018

Sessions says individuals who are victims of private crime not eligible for asylum

On June 11, 2018, Attorney General Jeff Sessions announced that people who are victims of personal crime are not elegable for asylum in the United States. U. S. Sessions said, "Our nation’s immigration laws provide for asylum to be granted to individuals who have been persecuted, or who have a well-founded fear of persecution, on account of their membership in a ‘particular social group,’ but most victims of personal crimes do not fit this definition—no matter how vile and reprehensible the crime perpetrated against them."

May 1, 2018

Texas and six other states file lawsuit to end DACA

On May 1, 2018, Texas and six other states filed a lawsuit because they believed that the continuation of the DACA program was unlawful. They argued that the program required them to spend more money on healthcare, law enforcement, and education. Also, the program increased competition for jobs.

On April 24, 2018, U.S. District Judge John Bates ruled that the Trump administration had to accept new DACA applications. Before this ruling the administration was only required to Previous rulings only required to process renewal requests. Another part of the ruling was that, the Trump administration had 90 days to explain their decision to end DACA

March 6, 2018

DOJ files lawsuit against California's immigration laws

ON March 6, 2018m U.S. Department of Justice (DOJ) filed a lawsuit against the State of California, Governor of California Jerry Brown, and Attorney General of California Xavier Becerra. They filed the lawsuit because they passed three laws in California prevent officials from enforcing federal immigration law. The U.S Department of Homeland Security asked a judgde to block the implementation of the laws.

According to a press release from the U.S Department of Justice,"The complaint contends that the laws in question are preempted by federal law and impermissibly target the Federal Government, and therefore violate the Supremacy Clause of the United States Constitution. As a result, the Justice Department is seeking to permanently enjoin these state statutes, which are contrary to federal law and interfere with federal immigration authorities’ ability to carry out their lawful duties."

    The lawsuit was against these Californian laws:
  • California SB 54: The law resticts local law enforemce officials from providing information to federal immigration authorities of the release date of immigrated. This law aslom violated 8 USC 1373, which promotes information sharing about immigration status.
  • California AB 450: The law prohibits emplohers from cooperating with federal immigration officials for worksite raids and other enforcement operations. It also, requires emplyers to notify their staff when their will be potential worksite enforcement.
  • California AB 103: The law stops a state run imspection and review scheme of the federal detention of immigrants. This law is California trying to relgulate immigrants' detention but that is not legal under the Constitutiom.
